Ratangad Fort

Hike up the rugged terrain to reach the majestic Ratangad Fort, perched atop a 2600-foot plateau. Witness panoramic vistas of the surrounding valleys and soak in the rich history etched in its remnants.

The trek to Ratangad is a moderate challenge, offering stunning views of the surrounding valleys and lush greenery. Climbers can choose between several routes, each with its own unique charm.

The fort's name has two interpretations: one claims it comes from Ratnabai Tandal, one of three sisters with forts named after them, while the other associates it with "Ratna", meaning "jewel", reflecting its majestic presence. Believed to be around 400 years old, it witnessed the Maratha reign under Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj and played a strategic role in defending the Konkan region.

HarishChandragad Wildlife Santuary

Embark on a nature trail through the verdant expanse of Harishchandragad Wildlife Sanctuary. Home to diverse flora and fauna, including leopards, sloth bears, and a variety of birds, the sanctuary promises an unforgettable encounter with nature's wonders.

The highlight of the sanctuary is the majestic Kalsubai Peak, the highest point in the Western Ghats of Maharashtra, standing at 5646 feet. Witnessing the panoramic views from its summit is an unforgettable experience.

The sanctuary is home to a rich assemblage of mammals, including leopards, jungle cats, palm civets, barking deer, and even the elusive Indian giant squirrels. Reptiles like monitor lizards, fan-throated lizards, and various snakes add to the ecological diversity. Birdwatchers will be delighted by the over 200 bird species found here, including white-necked storks, herons, ibises, and water bees.

Umbrella Falls

Hike through the lush trails amidst the Sahyadri ranges to reach the hidden gem - Umbrella Falls. The cascading water gushing over moss-covered rocks creates a picture-perfect natural shower, perfect for a refreshing dip.

Unlike most waterfalls, Umbrella Falls forms only during the monsoon season. As water overflows from the majestic Wilson Dam, it rushes through a small opening near its northern end, hitting a circular rock formation.
