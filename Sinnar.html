Ranekhan Wada

Take a journey back in time at the Ranekhan Wada, a majestic palace. Explore the grand halls, intricate courtyards, and ornate carvings, each reflecting the grandeur of the Maratha era. Immerse yourself in the rich history of the region and lose yourself in the opulent past.

Ranekhan Wada is a historic mansion located in the town of Shrirampur, Maharashtra. The mansion was built in the 18th century by the Ranekhan family, a prominent Maratha family.

The mansion is a two-story building with a large courtyard. The exterior of the mansion is made of red brick and has a distinctive arched entranceway. The interior of the mansion is decorated with intricate wood carvings and murals.

Gargoti Mineral Museum

Embark on a fascinating journey through the world of minerals at the Gargoti Mineral Museum. Discover an extensive collection mineral-specimens, including rare and precious stones, meteorites, and fossils. Learn about the geological history of the region and marvel at the diverse forms and colors of nature's hidden treasures.

The museum was founded by K.C. Pande, a local businessman who was passionate about minerals. Pande began collecting minerals as a hobby in the 1960s. Over the years, his collection grew to become one of the largest in India.

Gargoti Mineral Museum is a unique museum located in the town of Malgaon, Maharashtra. The museum is home to a collection of over 10,000 minerals from all over the world.

Chambhardari Water Falls

Seek rejuvenation at the cascading Chambhardari Waterfall, nestled amidst the Sahyadri hills. Take a refreshing dip in the cool waters, witness the misty rainbows shimmering in the sunlight, and lose yourself in the mesmerizing beauty of this natural wonder.

There are several ways to reach Chambhardari Falls. The most common way is to drive from Bhandardara. The falls are about a 15-minute drive from the town. There is also a hiking trail that leads to the falls.The falls are a popular spot for swimming, picnicking, and hiking. There is also a small temple located near the falls.

The best time to visit Chambhardari Falls is during the monsoon season (June-September), when the water levels are high and the falls are at their most dramatic. However, the falls are also beautiful during the rest of the year.
